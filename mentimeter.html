Mentimeter

Mentimeter is a helpful presentation-based digital tool that allows educators to make the most of its features for teaching, including quizzes, polls, and word clouds. If you're already using presentation tools in class, perhaps on a smart whiteboard or screen, this is a really powerful version of that which could help you in class.

The idea here is to make the creation of whole class, group, or individual quizzes and more, all very easy to create and use. As such, you can be more efficient with your time as an educator while students can easily engage with all the material you have on offer.

How to Use the Mentimeter?

Before the Presentation:

Sign Up or Log In

If you don't have a Mentimeter account, sign up for one. If you already have an account, log in.

Create a New Presentation

Start a new presentation in Mentimeter.

Choose a Presentation Template or Create Slides

Mentimeter provides various presentation templates, or you can create your own slides. Choose a template that fits your calculus content or create custom slides.

Add Interactive Elements

Use Mentimeter's interactive elements to engage your audience. Common interactive elements include multiple-choice questions, open-ended questions, word clouds, and quizzes.

Include Calculus Concepts

Design questions that cover calculus concepts you want to discuss. For example, you can create multiple-choice questions related to derivatives, integrals, limits, or specific problem-solving scenarios.

Insert Images and Graphs

Enhance your presentation by inserting images, diagrams, or graphs related to calculus concepts. You can upload images directly or use Slidebean's image search functionality.

Customize Design

Customize the design of your Mentimeter presentation. Choose colors, fonts, and styles that align with your presentation theme.

During the Presentation

Present with Mentimeter

Start presenting using Mentimeter. Share your screen or use a projector to display the Mentimeter slides to your audience.

Engage with Multiple-Choice Questions

Use Mentimeter's multiple-choice questions to gauge your audience's understanding of calculus concepts. Participants can submit their responses in real-time.

Discuss Responses

After participants respond to a question, take the opportunity to discuss the correct answer and address any misconceptions. Use this as a teaching moment.

Open-Ended Questions for Discussions

Incorporate open-ended questions to encourage discussions. Participants can provide written responses, and you can address these responses during the presentation.

Visualize Data

Utilize Mentimeter's visualization features to display live data. For example, you can create a word cloud to visualize common terms associated with a calculus concept.

Quiz Time

Utilize Mentimeter's visualization features to display live data. For example, you can create a word cloud to visualize common terms associated with a calculus concept.

After the Presentation

Access Results

Review the results and data collected during the Mentimeter presentation. Mentimeter allows you to access and analyze participant responses and engagement.

Export Data (if needed)

If you want to keep a record of the data collected, export the results from Mentimeter for future reference.
