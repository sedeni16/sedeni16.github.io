ZEARN

ZEARN

Zearn is an online-based math tuition tool for teaching students of varying ages about math. This is made by teachers and designed to be used by teachers. Everything is helpfully arranged by grade level and is curriculum-based, allowing teachers to give students learning stages with effective practice to ensure mastery.

Zearn is task-based but is also adaptive, so students can work their way through skills. It must be used in conjunction with teaching as this is more about working through tasks rather than being taught the concepts themselves.

HOW DOES ZEARN WORK?

Zearn can be setup for free by a teacher in order to get started right away. Students can then get invovled too, using this across a host of devices via the website.

Teachers should first teach the student or group the new mathematical technique which they wish for them to learn. Once they have this, it is then time to get onto Zearn to start practicing the process of that math skill.

When working on Zearn, students will be guided to help them progress independently of the teacher. Get something wrong and they can be given assistance or have choices lessened so as to help them toward the right answer -- while also understanding how they got to it.

HOW TO USED ZEARN?

1. **Create an Account:**

  • Visit the Zearn website and sign up for an account. You may need to provide some basic information.

  • Choose your role (e.g., teacher, student, parent) during the registration process.

2. **Select Your Role:**

Indicate whether you are a teacher, student, or parent. Teachers often have additional features and controls.

3. **Set Up Classes (For Teachers):**

If you're a teacher, set up your classes on the platform. This may involve entering student names and assigning them to specific classes.

4. **Navigate to the Curriculum:**

Access the curriculum section, where you'll find lessons organized by grade level and topic.

5. **Choose a Lesson:**

Select a specific lesson that aligns with the curriculum you're following.

6. **Follow the Lesson Flow:**

  • Each lesson typically includes various components such as instructional videos, interactive activities, and practice problems.

  • Follow the lesson flow, completing activities and exercises as instructed.

7. **Progress Tracking:**

Zearn often includes features for tracking student progress. Teachers can monitor student performance and identify areas that may need additional attention.

8. **Assign Homework or Additional Practice (For Teachers):**

Teachers may have the option to assign additional practice or homework to reinforce concepts outside of the classroom.

9. **Review Reports (For Teachers):**

Teachers can review reports to gain insights into individual student performance and overall class progress.

10. **Support and Resources:**

earn usually provides support resources, including FAQs, guides, and tutorials. Take advantage of these resources if you encounter any difficulties.
