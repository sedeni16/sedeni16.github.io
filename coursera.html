COURSERA

Coursera is an online learning platform founded by two Stanford University computer science professors. It offers thousands of online courses in partnership with over 200 of the world's leading universities and companies, including Yale, Princeton, Penn, Stanford, Google, and more.

HOW DOES COURSERA WORK?

To use Coursera, you'll need to create a free account (or log in using your Facebook, Google, or Apple account). Once you're signed up, you can take free and paid courses in a few different ways:

  • You can "Enroll for free" in one-off courses like Yale's Science of Well-Being or SUNY's How to Write a Resume.

  • You can also browse courses that are part of longer programs for free by clicking the enroll button and then "audit this course."

  • You can pay to take official MasterTrack and degree programs, which can cost a couple of thousand dollars, depending on the program.

WHAT ARE THE STEPS TO ENROLL IN COURSERA?

To enroll in a course:

STEP 1. Open the course description page by clicking on the course title.

STEP 2. Click Enroll.

STEP 3. Choose an option.

STEP 4. Read all instructions and follow any prompts you receive.

Once you’re enrolled, you can start taking a course at any time. If you come back to a course after a break, you may need to reset your assessment deadlines.

HOW TO USED COURSERA?

STEP 1. Type www.coursera.org in your web browser

STEP 2. The site requires all users to create a free account.

STEP 3. Complete the free account online form.

STEP 4. Coursera will send new users an e-mail to verify the account. Click on the verification link provided in the e-mail.

STEP 5. After signing in with account credentials, the user home page provides a series of steps on how to search and enroll in a course.

STEP 6. Courses are organized according to discipline. Alternatively, users can search the site using keywords of interest.

STEP 7. Select the course that is of interest. Browse the course page, peruse the syllabus, read the instructor’s biography. Click on the green enroll button.
