Desmos

Desmos

An online, interactive graphing calculator and mathematical software that enables users to graph, visualize, and manipulate mathematical functions and equations in both 2D and 3D. In the context of calculus, Desmos can help students and educators explore and understand calculus concepts through interactive and dynamic graphing and analysis.

How to Use the Desmos App?

STEP 1: Access Desmos

Open a web browser and go to the Desmos website. You can use Desmos for free without the need to download or install any software.

STEP 2: Familiarize Yourself with the Interface:

When you first open Desmos, you'll see a blank graphing canvas. On the left, you have a keypad for entering mathematical expressions. On the right, there's a sidebar with options for customization and analysis. Enter Calculus Functions: Use the keypad to enter the calculus function you want to explore. For example, you can enter functions like f(x), f'(x) for derivatives, and ∫(integral) for integrals. You can also enter piecewise functions or implicit equations.

STEP 3: Plot the Function

As you enter the function, Desmos will plot it on the graph. You can adjust the viewing window by zooming in/out or panning to focus on specific parts of the graph.

Customize the Graph:

Desmos allows you to customize the appearance of the graph. You can change line styles, colors, and labels to make your graph more informative and visually appealing.

STEP 4: Analyze Key Points

Identify critical points, maxima, and minima by finding where the derivative equals zero or is undefined. You can use the cursor to hover over points on the graph to see their coordinates.

Explore Tangent Lines:

To explore tangent lines, create a new function for the derivative and plot it alongside the original function. Use sliders to adjust the position of the tangent line and observe how it changes.

Calculate Integrals:

If you want to calculate integrals, enter the integral expression (e.g., ∫f(x)dx). Desmos will shade the area under the curve, and you can use sliders to adjust the integration limits.

STEP 5: Use Sliders for Interactivity

Add sliders to your graph to dynamically adjust parameters. For example, you can add a slider for 'a' and see how it impacts the behavior of the function.

STEP 6: Explore Limits

Set up limits to study the behavior of a function as it approaches a specific point. Desmos will help you visualize how the function values approach that point.

STEP 7: Save Your Work

You can save your graphs and settings for future reference or sharing. Desmos provides options to save your work as a link or as an image.

STEP 8: Share and Collaborate

If you're working on calculus problems as part of a class or study group, you can share your Desmos graph with others by providing them with the link. This allows for collaboration and seeking help from peers or instructors.

STEP 9: Explore Pre-Made Activities

Desmos offers a library of pre-made activities for calculus and other math topics. You can explore these activities for hands-on learning and problem-solving.

STEP 10: Learn from the Desmos Calculator Guide

Desmos provides a detailed guide on using the calculator, including advanced features and functions specific to calculus. You can refer to this guide for more in-depth assistance.
