Canva

Canva

Canva is a powerful design tool that can be used in education to create projects that not only look great but also help teach students the basics of digital design.

This is a free tool that allows students and teachers to work with photo editing, design layout, and more, all within an easy-to-use platform.

While this can be used by students to submit projects, it can also teach them how to work more creatively when laying out work. Teachers can also use the platform to create guidance, posters, and more for the classroom and beyond.

How to Use the Canva?

Sign Up or Log In

If you don't have a Canva account, sign up for one. If you already have an account, log in to access the platform.

Create a New Presentation

Once you're logged in, click on the "Create a design" button and select "Presentation." Alternatively, you can search for "presentation" in the Canva search bar.

Choose a Template

Canva offers a variety of presentation templates. Choose a template that suits the style and content of your calculus presentation. You can also start with a blank canvas.

Add Slides

Canva typically starts you off with one slide. Add more slides by clicking on the "Add a new page" button or using the slide sorter on the left side of the screen.

Customize Slide Layouts

Modify the layout of your slides by clicking on the "Layouts" tab on the left side. Choose a layout that best fits your content for each slide.

Add Calculus Content

Populate your slides with content related to calculus. Use text boxes to add explanations, bullet points, and titles. You can also use Canva's text options to emphasize key points.

Include Equations or Formulas

Use the text tool to add mathematical equations or formulas relevant to calculus. Canva may not have an equation editor, so you might need to use images of equations created elsewhere

Insert Visuals

Enhance your presentation by adding visuals like graphs, charts, or images. You can upload your own images or use Canva's extensive library of graphics.

Customize Design Elements

Customize the design elements such as colors, fonts, and backgrounds to match the theme of your calculus presentation. Consistency in design creates a professional look.

Animate Elements (if needed)

Canva allows you to animate certain elements on your slides. Explore the "Animate" option to add animations to text or graphics.

Preview Your Presentation

Before finalizing your presentation, preview it to ensure that all elements are correctly placed and the design looks cohesive.

Present Mode

Use Canva's "Present" mode to view your presentation in full screen. This gives you a better sense of how your audience will see it.

Save or Share

Save your presentation by clicking the "Download" button and selecting your preferred file format. You can also share your Canva presentation directly from the platform by clicking the "Share" button.

Download or Present

Once you are satisfied with your presentation, download the final version or present it directly using Canva's presentation mode.
