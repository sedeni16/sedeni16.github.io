WPS

WPS Office (an acronym for Writer, Presentation and Spreadsheets, previously known as Kingsoft Office) is an office suite for Microsoft Windows, macOS, Linux, iOS, Android, and HarmonyOS developed by Zhuhai-based Chinese software developer Kingsoft. It also comes pre-installed on Fire tablets.

How to Use the WPS?

Download and Install WPS Office:

If you haven't already, download and install WPS Office on your computer or device.

Open WPS Presentation

Launch the WPS Presentation application.

Choose a Template

Select a presentation template that suits your needs. You might find templates specifically designed for educational presentations or choose a generic one and customize it.

Title Slide

Create a title slide with a title that clearly indicates the topic of your calculus presentation. Include your name and any relevant details.

Content Slides

Create slides to cover the main topics of your calculus presentation. Each slide should focus on a specific concept or point.

Text and Equations

Use the text feature to enter explanations and bullet points. If you need to include mathematical equations, use the equation editor or insert images of mathematical expressions.

Graphs and Charts

Use the chart and graph tools to visually represent calculus concepts. For example, you might include graphs of functions, curves, or other graphical representations.

Animations (if needed)

If your presentation requires animations to illustrate step-by-step processes or transitions between concepts, use the animation features provided by WPS Presentation.

Speaker Notes

Utilize the speaker notes feature to include additional information or reminders for yourself during the presentation. These notes won't be visible to the audience.

Practice Presenting with WPS Office Remote (if applicable)

If you are presenting using a remote control, WPS Office has a feature called WPS Office Remote that allows you to control presentations from your smartphone. Familiarize yourself with this feature if you plan to use it.

Save and Share

After the presentation, save any changes and consider sharing the presentation file or exporting it to a format compatible with other devices.
