Piktochart

Piktochart is a cloud-based application that allows users to easily create infographics. Users can embed interactive maps, charts, videos, and hyperlinks in a Piktochart infographic. To create an infographic, you start by choosing a blank or pre-designed template or theme, and then add information and media. Piktochart provides a library of icons, images, fonts, and other design tools. Icons and images are organized by subject, including education, entertainment, people, and shapes. Once completed, each infographic can be saved for future editing, and the finished product can be exported as a PNG, JPEG, or PDF file.

How to Use the Piktochart?

Sign Up or Log In

If you don't have a Piktochart account, sign up for one. If you already have an account, log in.

Choose a Presentation Template

Once you're logged in, select a presentation template from Piktochart's template library. Look for templates that suit the style and content you want for your calculus presentation.

Edit Template

Customize the template to fit your calculus content. Replace placeholder text with your own, add images, and adjust the layout as needed.

Create Sections and Slides

Divide your calculus presentation into sections, and create slides for each section. This helps in organizing your content logically.

Add Text and Formulas

Use text boxes to add explanations and bullet points. If your presentation includes mathematical formulas, you can use the text editor to input mathematical symbols and equations.

Insert Images and Graphs

Enhance your presentation by inserting images, diagrams, or graphs related to calculus concepts. Piktochart allows you to upload images and customize their placement.

Use Icons and Graphics

Explore Piktochart's library of icons and graphics to visually represent concepts in your calculus presentation. This can make your slides more engaging and easier to understand.

Choose Fonts and Colors

Customize fonts and colors to match your style and ensure readability. Consistent use of fonts and colors helps in maintaining a professional and cohesive look.

Animate Elements (if needed)

Depending on your preferences and the content of your presentation, consider using Piktochart's animation features to add movement to certain elements.

Preview Your Presentation

Before finalizing your presentation, preview it to ensure that all elements are correctly positioned, and there are no formatting issues.

Save Your Work

Save your work regularly to avoid losing any changes. Piktochart typically autosaves your work, but it's a good practice to save manually as well.

Download Your Presentation (Optional)

If you need to present offline or use a different platform, you can download your Piktochart presentation as a PDF or image file.
